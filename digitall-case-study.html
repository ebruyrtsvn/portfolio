The Problem

300+ agents starting each day without a clear direction

DigitALL is Allianz Turkey's internal B2B platform used exclusively by insurance agents to manage their full portfolio — policies, customers, performance targets, sales opportunities, and rewards. I was the sole designer on this platform with full ownership across research, design, and stakeholder alignment.

When I joined, the homepage dashboard was a collection of 6 equal-weight tiles — Web Referans, Müşteri, Hasar, Satış Fırsatları, Performans, Kampanyalar — arranged with no visual hierarchy, no prioritization logic, and no way to understand at a glance what actually needed attention that day.

I open the dashboard and I still don't know where to start. Everything looks equally important, so nothing feels important.
No HierarchyAll 6 tiles were identical in size and weight. Urgent renewals and cancelled policies were buried alongside low-priority campaign content.
Data BlindnessPerformance numbers were raw text — no visual benchmarks, no progress context. Agents couldn't tell if %80.9 was good or alarming without manual calculation.
FragmentationSatış Fırsatları, Müşteri Kartı, and Ödül Programları were scattered across disconnected widgets with no logical grouping.
NoiseThe Kampanyalar widget frequently showed "Güncellenecektir" placeholder text, eroding agent trust in the entire dashboard.

Design Constraints

  • Power users, but time is scarce in agent workflows
  • Existing backend data structure maintained — no new data sources
  • Parallel development sprints — design shipped incrementally
  • Multiple profiles: high-volume veterans vs. onboarding agents

Scope of Impact

  • 300+ insurance agents as daily active users
  • Primary operational tool — no workarounds available
  • Agent KPIs directly tied to management performance reviews
  • Mobile gap identified during research → fed into strategic roadmap
User Research

Listening to agents — finding the real friction points

Before any wireframes, I ran structured interviews and contextual observation sessions with a cross-section of agents — from high-volume veterans managing 500+ policies to newly onboarded agents navigating the platform for the first time. The goal: understand what the first 5 minutes of their workday actually looked like, and where the legacy dashboard was failing them.

“

I need to check 3 separate widgets just to understand my sales situation. By the time I've pieced it together, I've already lost 10 minutes. On a busy day, that matters.

Senior Insurance Agent — 8+ years on the platform
Pain Points Surfaced
  • No way to see priority tasks without clicking through multiple tiles
  • Performance percentage conveyed nothing without a target visual
  • Renewal alerts mixed with birthday notifications — no urgency differentiation
  • Cross-sell opportunities not linked to customer profiles
  • Dashboard felt overwhelming — "too much, all at once"
  • No mobile access — agents couldn't check status while with clients
Design Insights
  • Agents needed a "what do I do TODAY" section at the top — always
  • Visual KPI rings would replace mental math with instant clarity
  • Group items by urgency level — action items vs. informational
  • Ödül Programları was motivational — agents wanted it visible, not buried
  • Announcements needed to be prominent but non-blocking
  • Mobile coverage is a critical strategic gap — escalated to roadmap
The Solution

From complexity to clarity — a priority-first dashboard

Rather than rearranging the same tiles, I restructured the entire information architecture around a single question: what does an agent need to act on first? The redesigned dashboard answers that within 3 seconds of opening the platform.

The new layout is built on three layers: Today's Priorities (immediate actions required), Performance Context (how am I tracking against targets), and Opportunities & Rewards (what else should I leverage). Each layer has a clear visual identity, allowing agents to scan vertically and jump to what they need instantly.

Layer 01
Bugün Öncelikli — Priority Strip
4 priority counters pinned to the top: policies to renew, auto-cancellations, cancellation letters, and birthday customers. Color-coded, always visible, one click to action.
Layer 02
Performans — Visual KPI Hub
Ring charts replace raw percentages. Target vs. actual prim, policies produced, and year-over-year premium change — all benchmarked visually. No more mental calculation.
Layer 03
Satış, Müşteri & Ödül
Cross-sell opportunities, customer portfolio summary, and loyalty program status grouped logically in the lower grid. Motivational data surfaced clearly.
Design Process

From agent frustration to a shipped dashboard

01
Stakeholder alignment & scoping
Mapped the business context — what metrics mattered to management, what KPIs agents were accountable for, and what data the backend could surface. Aligned with product and dev leads to scope the redesign as a phased initiative rather than a full rebuild.
02
Agent research & contextual interviews
Conducted structured interviews across seniority levels. Observed how agents used the dashboard in real sessions — noting where attention fell, where they hesitated, and what mental models they'd built to compensate for interface shortcomings.
03
Information architecture restructure
Defined a 3-layer hierarchy before touching any UI: actionable priorities → performance context → opportunities. Mapped each existing widget to its new position and identified what to consolidate, what needed visual upgrade, and what was noise to eliminate.
04
Component design & visual system
Designed within the DigitALL component library. Introduced ring charts for KPI visualization — the highest-impact single change. Every widget had defined empty, loading, and error states.
05
Validation & iteration with agent panel
Ran prototype walkthroughs with 8 agents — 4 veterans, 4 newer agents. Measured time-to-identify-priority-task before and after. Iterated on the priority strip and Performans widget density. Final design passed stakeholder review in the second iteration.
06
Mobile gap identification & roadmap input
Research surfaced a critical unmet need: agents frequently needed to check their dashboard while meeting clients in the field, but the platform had no mobile experience. Documented this as a strategic product gap and successfully advocated for its inclusion in the 2025 roadmap.

Reflection

What I'd do differently

What worked well

Starting with research and refusing to touch the UI until I understood the actual workflow proved critical. The 3-layer hierarchy wasn't a design preference — it came directly from observing how agents processed information. The mobile gap identification was also a significant outcome that went beyond the original brief.

What I'd approach differently

With more time, I would have pushed for a longitudinal study — measuring dashboard engagement patterns over several months rather than relying on snapshot surveys. I'd also have designed personalisation options for the widget grid, since veteran agents and onboarding agents have meaningfully different priority needs.
